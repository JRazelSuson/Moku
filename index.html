About Chef Cris

On August 15, 2005, Cristeta Pacia Comerford made history as the first woman, Asian, and Filipino to serve as Executive Chef at the White House. Her journey from Manila to Washington D.C. is a testament to skill, grace under pressure, and the power of representation.

“I Googled myself and articles in many languages said the same thing – the White House chef is female. It was surreal, not just for me but for every young culinarian out there.”

Career Highlights

  • Executive Chef, White House (2005–present).
  • Crafts menus for events from intimate dinners to state banquets for royalty and Congressional picnics for 3,000.
  • Chosen from 450 applicants; her flawless state dinner for the Indian Prime Minister doubled as her audition, silently judged by First Lady Laura Bush.
  • Balances the demands of a living museum with the comfort of the First Family.

Family & Culinary Roots

Cris credits her culinary instincts to her mother, who effortlessly fed a blended family of 11 children. Today, the Comerford household keeps the tradition alive:

  • Danielle, her daughter, prepared a four-course Mother’s Day meal at age eight.
  • John, her husband and fellow chef, stepped back from his own career to support Cris and nurture their daughter.
“Whatever you’re working for, you work to share with your family.”

Filipino Flavor in the White House

Chef Cris loves blending Filipino flavors into classic dishes—think adobo-stuffed roulades or hints of longganisa in small bites. At home, a rule stands: if a dish takes more than ten minutes, it’s too much work!

Breakfast favorites include:

  • Tocino
  • Longganisa
  • Corned beef
  • Garlic fried rice

Looking Ahead

Many call her career the pinnacle, but Chef Comerford sees only stepping stones:

“Every task—menial or grand—deserves the best of your God-given ability. There is no real pinnacle, only the next opportunity to serve.”
