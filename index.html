Biography

Cristeta Comerford is the first woman and first person of Asian descent to serve as the Executive Chef at the White House. Born in the Philippines, she moved to the United States in her early twenties and pursued a career in culinary arts.

Career Highlights

  • Appointed by First Lady Laura Bush in 2005.
  • Has served under multiple presidents, including George W. Bush, Barack Obama, Donald Trump, and Joe Biden.
  • Specializes in healthy, multicultural cuisine and sustainable cooking practices.

Fun Facts

  • Graduated from the University of the Philippines Diliman.
  • Worked in various prestigious hotels and restaurants before joining the White House kitchen in 1995.
  • Loves to fuse Filipino flavors into modern American dishes.
