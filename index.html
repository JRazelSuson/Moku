Cristeta Comerford

White House Executive Chef

Biography

Cristeta Comerford is the first woman and the first Asian-American to hold the position of White House Executive Chef. She was born in the Philippines and moved to the U.S. to pursue her culinary career.

Career Highlights

  • Joined the White House in 1995.
  • Appointed Executive Chef in 2005 by First Lady Laura Bush.
  • Continued to serve under Presidents Obama, Trump, and Biden.

Fun Facts

  • She studied food technology at the University of the Philippines Diliman.
  • She has a talent for integrating global flavors, especially Filipino cuisine, into American dishes.
