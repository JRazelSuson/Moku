Cristeta Comerford

White House Executive Chef

Biography Career Fun Facts

Biography

Cristeta Comerford is the first woman and first person of Asian descent to serve as the Executive Chef at the White House. Born in the Philippines, she moved to the U.S. in her twenties and began her journey in the culinary arts.

Career Highlights

  • Joined the White House kitchen in 1995.
  • Appointed Executive Chef in 2005 by First Lady Laura Bush.
  • Continues to serve under multiple U.S. Presidents.
  • Known for healthy, diverse, and globally inspired dishes.

Fun Facts

  • Studied food technology at the University of the Philippines Diliman.
  • Loves to fuse Filipino flavors into modern cuisine.
  • Has appeared on various food and cultural TV segments.
